Origins

The wandering dude, also known as the wandering jew, silver inch, or tradescantia zebrina, is a native plant from Mexico. They are grown as houseplants in North America. Because they are considered invasive in warm outdoor regions, keep them in indoor spaces as houseplants.

How to care for the Wandering Dude Plant

The Tradescantia Zebrina thrives in bright indirect sunlight. In fact, the more sunlight it gets, the more purple the plant gets. However, try to avoid bright direct light as it can burn the delicate leaves of the plant.

My Tradescantia is placed right in front of an east-facing window where it recieves plenty of indirect sunlight during the day. Due to the abundance of bright light it recieves, I notice that it grows extremely quickly and the purple is much more vibrant on the side that faces the window. If you want an even-looking plant, I suggest rotating the plant every one to two weeks to ensure each side gets enough sunlight.

Never let the soil of a Tradescantia dry out completely. You want to make sure the soil is consistently moist. This most likely means watering every week during hotter seasons and once every two to three weeks in the colder climates. But this measurement will definitely vary so your best bet is to check soil moisture levels every week to see if it needs to be watered.

During summer I tend to give my plant a thorough watering every week during since it gets quite a bit of sunlight for long hours of the day. It also tends to get hot indoors so the soil becomes dry faster than it does in the winter. During the winter, my plant does not recieve as high intensity sunlight and it resides in a much cooler indoor environment, thus, I tend to water it every two to three weeks. No matter the season, I use my soil moister meter to check up on my plant every week just in case I need to water it or skip watering it for the week.

Variations of the Tradescantia

The Tradescantia Nanouk is a rare variation of the Tradescantia species. The varigation is slighty more pink and there are more stripes than compared to the Zebrina.
The Tradescantia Pallida is a deep shade of purple with much longer leaves than the average Tradescantia Zebrina. It's more commonly known as the Purple Heart.
The Tradescantia Variegata has no purple or pink varigation. Instead it's mainly green, yellow, and white stripes
